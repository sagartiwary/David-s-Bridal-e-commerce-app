Why are wedding dresses white?

+

The origin of the white wedding dress tradition dates back to 1840, when Queen Victoria made a statement by wearing a white lace gown at her royal wedding. Previously, wedding gowns had come in a wide variety of colors, both for practicality as well as style. White was rarely worn, so the Queen's choice sparked instant notice and set a new trend of white wedding gowns that endures to this day.

What is the most popular wedding dress silhouette?

+

The most popular wedding dress silhouette is the A-line wedding dress because of its universally flattering shape. The nipped-in waist hugs your curves while the skirt flares gently for a comfortable and elegant style that's a timeless favorite.

What to wear under a wedding dress

+

Brides typically wear slips to keep their skirt in place, adhesive bras for strapless gowns, and shaper panties to create a smooth, secure, and confident ensemble. One helpful tip: If you have undergarments that you plan on using under your dress, bring them to your appointment when you are dress shopping.

How to bustle a wedding dress

+

This is a prefect job for your maid of honor, your mom or your mother-in-law. Depending on the shape of your gown, most dresses can have hooks added by alterations experts designed to pick up the train of the gown as they tuck under the silhouette itself. The different bustle styles include French bustle, under bustle, royal bustle, victorian bustle, ballroom bustle, and bow bustle.
